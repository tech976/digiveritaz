Our UI UX Design Process

03

Discovery & User Research

User interviews, contextual inquiry, stakeholder workshops, and competitor teardowns produce the foundation. We focus on understanding the actual jobs users are trying to do and where current solutions fail them.

04

Information Architecture & User Flows

Card sorting, sitemap definition, and end-to-end user flow diagrams ensure the product structure makes sense before any pixel-level work begins. Most usability problems are architecture problems in disguise.

05

Wireframing & Low-Fidelity Prototyping

Greyscale wireframes test layout, content hierarchy, and core flows quickly. We iterate fast at this stage when changes are cheap — never at high-fidelity when changes cost days of design time.

06

High-Fidelity UI Design & Visual Craft

Pixel-perfect Figma designs that align with your brand, balance accessibility with delight, and translate cleanly to development. Every screen ships in light/dark mode, mobile/tablet/desktop, and edge-state variants.

07

Prototyping & Usability Testing

Interactive prototypes tested with 5–8 real users surface usability problems before development. Findings are documented, prioritised, and resolved in design — not after expensive engineering work.

08

Design System & Developer Handoff

Component libraries, design tokens, spacing systems, and Figma developer mode handoff — built so your engineers can ship faster and future design work compounds rather than starting from scratch.

Design Principles We Apply Across Every Project

Clarity beats cleverness

users should never have to think about how to use the interface

Defaults matter

most users never change defaults, so design defaults that serve them

Reduce cognitive load

show what users need now, hide what they don't

Respect attention

no dark patterns, no manipulative design, no friction for friction's sake

Design for failure states

error messages, empty states, and loading are 50% of UX

Accessibility from day one

WCAG AA compliance is the floor, not a stretch goal

Performance is design

slow interfaces are bad design regardless of how they look

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you only design or also handle development?
We are primarily a design agency, but we partner closely with development teams (in-house at clients, our engineering partners, or independent shops). For end-to-end product builds, we provide handoff-ready Figma files, design systems, and developer support throughout implementation.
How long does a typical UI UX project take?
MVP design typically takes 4–6 weeks; full product redesigns 8–12 weeks; complex multi-product platforms 12–20 weeks. Timelines depend on scope, research depth, number of platforms, and stakeholder review cycles.
Do you conduct actual user research or just design what we ask for?
Yes — research is foundational to everything we do. Without research, design becomes opinion-driven decoration. We conduct user interviews, usability testing, and behavioural analysis as part of every meaningful engagement.
How do you price UI UX design projects?
Most projects are priced based on scope and complexity rather than hourly rates. MVP design starts around ₹2.5 lakhs; full product designs range ₹6–15 lakhs; ongoing design retainers from ₹1.5 lakhs per month. We provide fixed-scope quotes after a discovery call.
Can you work with our existing design system or do we need a new one?
Both. We can extend and improve your existing design system, or build a new one from the ground up if your current system is fragmented or outdated. Building on existing systems is usually faster and preserves visual continuity.
