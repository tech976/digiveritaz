How we work

Five disciplines that turn native spend into qualified visitors.

Each runs in lockstep so the campaign compounds — instead of just spending budget.

01

Audience & content alignment

We start by matching the campaign content to the readers it's meant to attract — interest segments, contextual signals and publisher categories. Native fails when the landing content doesn't earn the click; we make sure the read is worth it.

02

Headline & creative testing

Editorial-quality headlines and native-looking imagery, tested in batches of 15-20 at a time. We optimise for honest CTR and post-click engagement — never clickbait that destroys brand trust on landing.

03

Publisher whitelisting & brand safety

Native networks include both premium and very low-quality inventory. We curate domain whitelists, exclude misinformation categories and audit placements weekly so your brand never lands next to content it shouldn't.

Smart bid management at placement level — pushing budget toward inventory that converts and frequency-capping so the same reader isn't shown the same recommendation seven times in a session.

05

Reporting & conversion attribution

Server-side conversion tracking, post-click session quality and attribution back to leads or revenue. Monthly reviews tell you exactly what worked, what's being killed and how next month gets sharper.

FAQs

Questions teams ask before they sign.

Is native advertising the same as sponsored content?
They are closely related. Native ads appear in feed or recommendation widgets and link to an article or landing page. Sponsored content is a full editorial piece hosted on a publisher's site. Both form part of a native advertising strategy.
What budgets work for native advertising?
Native campaigns typically need a minimum of ₹50,000 per month to generate sufficient data for meaningful optimisation and reach.
How does native advertising compare to Google Display Network?
Native ads typically deliver higher engagement rates and longer time-on-site than GDN — because users are reading rather than browsing when they see them. GDN typically delivers cheaper raw clicks. Content engagement and consideration favour native; broad retargeting and awareness favour GDN; most sophisticated programmes use both.
How do you keep our brand off low-quality publisher sites?
Every campaign launches with a curated domain whitelist and category-level exclusions for conspiracy, misinformation and clickbait inventory. We audit placements weekly and remove low-quality publishers from spend.
What does a good native landing page look like?
A content page — not a homepage. Editorial structure, substantive read, and a soft CTA that follows the value the reader expected from the headline. Pages that match expectation convert; pages that bait-and-switch destroy brand trust.
