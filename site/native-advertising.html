Banner blindness has rendered traditional display advertising substantially less effective over the past decade. Users have learned to ignore banner positions, install ad blockers, and scroll past anything that looks like advertising. Native advertising solves the attention problem differently — by appearing in the content stream rather than alongside it, and by reading more like editorial content than interruption marketing. Done well, native ads earn engagement banners cannot; done badly, they generate clicks from frustrated users who feel deceived.

As a native advertising services provider in India, DV Digital runs native ad campaigns across Outbrain, Taboola, Colombia, MGID, and emerging native networks. We focus on the discipline that actually drives native performance — strong content marketing alignment, headline craft that earns clicks honestly, landing pages that match expectation, and brand-safety vigilance that protects reputation while scaling reach.

Common Native Advertising Mistakes We Avoid

Clickbait headlines that drive clicks but destroy brand trust on landing

Sending native ad traffic to homepages instead of content pages

Ignoring placement quality — native networks include both premium and very low-quality inventory

Treating native like display — different platforms require different creative approaches

Optimising for click-through rate alone instead of downstream conversion

Insufficient creative variety — testing 2 ads forever instead of 20 ads monthly

Skipping brand safety configuration and ending up on conspiracy or misinformation sites

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Is native advertising the same as sponsored content?
They are closely related. Native ads appear in feed or recommendation widgets and link to an article or landing page. Sponsored content is a full editorial piece hosted on a publisher's site. Both form part of a native advertising strategy.
What budgets work for native advertising?
Native campaigns typically need a minimum of ₹50,000 per month to generate sufficient data for meaningful optimisation and reach.
How does native advertising compare to Google Display Network?
Native ads typically deliver higher engagement rates and longer time-on-site than GDN — because users are reading rather than browsing when they see them. GDN typically delivers cheaper raw clicks. The right choice depends on your goal: content engagement and consideration favour native; broad retargeting and awareness favour GDN; most sophisticated programmes use both for different funnel stages.
