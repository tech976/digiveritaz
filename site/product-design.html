We've helped founders cut MVP scope in half, shipped products from blank Figma to production in under 12 weeks, and redesigned legacy interfaces that lifted activation by 30–60% — without rewriting the backend.

Most agencies still treat product design as the visual layer applied at the end of engineering. That model fails because every meaningful product decision — what to build, who it's for, how it monetises, what gets measured — is fundamentally a design decision.

For early-stage founders, the leverage of getting product design right is enormous. The right MVP scope and user experience is often the difference between Series A traction and a quiet pivot — and we've shipped both outcomes enough times to know what separates them.

How we work

From blank Figma to shipped product — in five disciplined steps.

Five disciplines we run in lockstep so what ships is what was scoped, and what was scoped is what users actually wanted.

01

Discovery & product strategy

Founder workshops, user interviews, market analysis and Jobs-To-Be-Done framing. We produce a clear answer to the only question that matters at this stage: what specifically should we build, for whom, and why now?

02

Problem framing & opportunity sizing

We pressure-test the problem before we design a solution — sizing the audience, mapping competitive products, and stress-testing willingness-to-pay so the product brief survives contact with the market.

03

Concepting & MVP scoping

From discovery, we narrow to the smallest set of features that delivers core value and lets you start learning from real users. Most MVPs are still over-scoped on first pass — we help you ship faster by saying no.

04

Design & build sprints

Two-week sprints with paired design + engineering — wireframes, prototypes, production UI, components and motion specs reviewed and shipped together. No "throw it over the wall" handoffs.

05

Launch, measure & iterate

Instrumentation goes in before launch, not after. We watch activation, time-to-value and retention curves with you weekly, then turn the data into the next sprint of design decisions.

Measurement

Pixels are the input. Product KPIs are the output.

Six KPIs we report on every cycle so design activity always ladders back to product outcomes — not pages of Figma shipped.

01

Activation rate

% of new users who complete the core action that defines product value — the single most important early-stage metric.

02

Time-to-value

How quickly a new user reaches the "aha" moment — minutes, not days, is the bar for almost every consumer product.

03

Retention curve

Week-1, week-4 and week-12 retention by cohort — the curve that decides whether your product is a vitamin or a painkiller.

04

NPS / CSAT

Qualitative pulse on perceived value — paired with the quantitative metrics so the why never gets lost behind the what.

05

Feature adoption

% of active users touching each major feature — what's loved, what's ignored, what gets cut from the next release.

06

North-star metric

The one number that best represents delivered customer value — every other KPI ladders back to this single line.

FAQs

Questions founders ask before they sign.

Do you work with pre-funded startups or only Series A+?
Both. A meaningful portion of our work is with pre-seed and seed-stage founders — often the engagements where great design has the highest leverage. Pricing models range from fixed-scope MVP design to hybrid retainer + equity arrangements for select bets.
How is product design different from UI/UX design?
UI/UX design typically focuses on existing product scope and improving the interface and experience. Product design includes those disciplines but extends earlier — into product strategy, scope definition, and ongoing iteration based on usage data. Product designers are essentially product managers who design.
Will you sign an NDA before discussing our product idea?
Yes — we sign NDAs as standard practice before any substantive product discussion. Founder ideas deserve protection and we treat early-stage discussions with appropriate confidentiality.
Can you help with our pitch deck and investor demo?
Yes. Investor-ready prototypes, pitch deck product visuals, and demo flow design are part of what we do regularly for fundraising stage clients. Strong product visuals materially improve investor conversion in Indian and global VC contexts.
What does product design typically cost for an MVP?
Most MVP design engagements range from ₹3 to ₹12 lakhs depending on scope, platforms (web, iOS, Android), and research depth. We provide fixed-scope proposals after an initial discovery call.
