FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What metrics do you track for performance marketing?
Core metrics: cost per lead, cost per acquisition, return on ad spend, conversion rate, and click-through rate. We also track pipeline contribution and revenue attribution where CRM integration is in place.
How is performance marketing different from traditional advertising?
Traditional advertising charges for placement or reach regardless of outcome. Performance marketing charges based on measurable actions — clicks, leads, or sales — making ROI directly calculable and campaigns directly accountable.
What's the difference between performance marketing and digital marketing?
Performance marketing is a subset of digital marketing focused exclusively on measurable, ROI-accountable activity — paid acquisition, direct-response campaigns, and channels where every rupee is tied to an outcome. Digital marketing is broader, including brand-building, content marketing, SEO, and PR where direct measurement is harder. Most healthy marketing strategies combine performance marketing with brand investment, not choose one over the other.
What's the minimum budget for meaningful performance marketing?
Across all channels, ₹2 lakhs/month is roughly the floor where performance marketing economics start working — below that, agency fees consume too much of spend and machine learning algorithms have too little data. Single-channel programmes can work at ₹1 lakh/month for tightly-targeted campaigns. Larger budgets unlock multi-channel optimisation and faster learning cycles.
How is performance marketing priced?
Either as percentage of spend (typically 10–18% for accounts above ₹3 lakhs/month) or flat retainers (₹50,000–₹3 lakhs/month) for smaller or strategy-heavy engagements. Some agencies offer hybrid models with performance bonuses tied to ROAS or CPA targets. We're transparent about which model applies and why for each engagement.
