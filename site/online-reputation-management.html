How we work

Five disciplines. One reputation system.

The structured framework we use to map, rebuild and protect online reputations — every engagement, every client.

01

Reputation audit & baseline mapping

We map every public surface where your brand appears — Google search results, review platforms, social media, news mentions, forums and AI search engine responses. From this baseline we build a prioritised remediation and growth plan tied to the SERP positions and platforms doing the most damage.

02

Review acquisition & response management

Active workflow for soliciting genuine reviews from satisfied customers, responding professionally to every review (positive or negative) and resolving complaints publicly — so future buyers see your service quality in real time, not just the loudest unhappy voice.

03

Negative-content suppression via SERP

Where damaging content cannot be removed at source, we build and rank positive owned and earned content that pushes negative results below page 1 — where 95%+ of searchers never look. Legitimate positive ranking, never spam content farms.

04

Wikipedia, Quora & forum management

Establishing and maintaining accurate Wikipedia presence where notability allows, authoritative Quora answers from verified accounts, and proactive forum engagement on Reddit and category sub-communities — to surface positive context around your brand.

05

Social listening & crisis response

24/7 monitoring of brand mentions across platforms with defined escalation protocols. When a crisis emerges we activate response within hours, not days — minimising spread and protecting long-term equity. Defamation, doxxing and impersonation get legal-coordinated takedowns.

Common scenarios

Six situations we resolve every month.

Reputation problems come in patterns. Here are the six we handle most often — and the legitimate playbook for each.

Negative news ranking

A damaging news article ranking on page 1 for your brand or executive name — suppressed by ranking positive owned and earned content above it.

Review cluster damage

A cluster of damaging reviews suppressing star rating — rebalanced with legitimate post-purchase outreach to genuinely happy customers.

Ex-employee complaints

Disgruntled ex-employee complaints on Glassdoor, AmbitionBox or LinkedIn — answered professionally, with current employee voice amplified.

Competitor attacks

Competitor-driven black-hat SEO attacks and disinformation — documented, disavowed where applicable, defended through legitimate authority.

Forum threads gaining traction

Unfounded Reddit or forum threads gaining search visibility — addressed with verified-account responses, not deletion attempts that escalate.

Defamation & impersonation

Defamation, doxxing or impersonation incidents — coordinated takedowns with legal counsel, DMCA notices and platform-policy enforcement.

FAQs

Questions teams ask before starting ORM.

Can you remove negative content from Google search results?
Sometimes — we have several legitimate avenues including direct platform removal requests, copyright-based DMCA takedowns where applicable, right-to-be-forgotten requests, and coordination with legal counsel for defamation matters. Where removal is not possible, we suppress damaging content by ranking positive owned content above it on the SERP.
How long does ORM take to show results?
Visible SERP changes typically begin within 4–8 weeks. Significant reputation transformation — measured by SERP composition, review volume and sentiment scores — usually takes 4–6 months of consistent work. Severely damaged reputations can take 6–12 months to fully repair.
Will you buy fake positive reviews to balance the negative ones?
Absolutely not. Fake reviews are unethical, violate platform policies, are detectable by review platforms and Google, and ultimately damage trust further when discovered. We build review volume through legitimate post-purchase outreach to genuine customers who had positive experiences.
What does an ORM engagement cost?
Engagements range from ₹50,000 per month for ongoing reputation maintenance to ₹2,50,000+ per month for active crisis recovery and SERP cleanup. Pricing depends on the severity of the situation, the volume of negative content, and the timeline objectives.
Do you work with individuals or only companies?
Both. We run extensive ORM engagements for senior executives, doctors, lawyers and high-net-worth individuals where personal name search results matter — alongside our brand-level engagements. Personal reputation work is particularly time-sensitive and we handle it with discretion.
