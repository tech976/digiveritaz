A website that loads in under two seconds, ranks on the right queries and converts the right visitors is a compounding sales asset — not a cost centre. That's the only kind we ship.

Most agency websites look great in a Figma flyover and fall apart in production: bloated themes, render-blocking scripts, missing schema, broken canonical tags, and a CMS the marketing team is too scared to touch. The result is a beautiful site that fails the only two audiences that matter — buyers and search engines.

How we work

From discovery to launch — five disciplined stages.

A predictable, milestone-driven build process so you always know what's happening this week, what's shipping next week, and what's on the runway after launch.

01

Discovery & strategy

Business goals, target audiences, conversion paths, content inventory, integration map and search demand mapped before a single pixel is drawn. Every site starts as a brief, not a template.

02

Information architecture & wireframes

Sitemap, page hierarchy, URL structure, internal linking and low-fidelity wireframes signed off before visual design — so the structure works whether the styling is bold or minimal.

03

Design & component system

Brand-aligned visual design backed by a tokenised component library — typography, spacing, colour, motion — so every future page stays consistent without designer escalation.

04

Development & integration

Frontend, backend and CMS built with performance budgets, accessibility checks, schema markup, analytics and CRM integrations wired in before content load — not after.

05

Launch & post-launch support

Pre-launch QA, redirect mapping, search console handover, then a 3-month post-launch period covering bug fixes, performance monitoring, uptime alerts and minor content edits.

Measurement

Pixels are the input. Performance, ranking and revenue are the output.

Six KPIs we benchmark before launch and track for the life of every site we ship.

01

Lighthouse score

Performance, accessibility, best practices and SEO benchmarked at every build stage — targeting 95+ on mobile.

02

LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)

Hero render time on mobile budgeted under 2.5 seconds across templates — the biggest Core Web Vital signal.

03

CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)

Layout stability budgeted under 0.05 — no jumping CTAs, no images popping in mid-scroll, no font-flash shift.

04

INP (Interaction to Next Paint)

Interactivity latency tracked under 200 ms — every tap, scroll and form interaction responds without jank.

05

Time-to-launch

Calendar weeks from briefing to live site, tracked against scope class — marketing site, e-commerce or web app.

06

Post-launch uptime

Uptime monitoring, vulnerability scanning and Core Web Vitals tracking continuing through the support window.

FAQs

Questions teams ask before they sign.

What CMS do you build websites on?
We primarily build on WordPress and Webflow, with custom development for projects requiring bespoke functionality. Platform recommendations are based on your team's capabilities and your site's functional requirements.
How long does a website project take?
A standard business website takes 6 to 10 weeks from briefing to launch. More complex sites with custom functionality or large content volumes take 12 to 16 weeks.
WordPress, Webflow, or custom — which platform should we choose?
It depends on requirements. WordPress for content-heavy sites, blog-driven SEO programmes, and complex content workflows. Webflow for design-led marketing sites where the team needs visual editing without code. Custom (React, Next.js) for performance-critical sites, complex applications, or specialised functionality. We recommend the right platform after understanding your specific situation, not from agency preference.
How long does a website project typically take?
Standard marketing websites: 8–14 weeks from kickoff to launch. Larger e-commerce or content-heavy sites: 14–22 weeks. Custom web applications with bespoke functionality: 16–32 weeks. Timelines depend on content readiness, stakeholder review cycles, and scope clarity at kickoff. Aggressive timelines are possible for focused builds with pre-existing content.
What does a website cost?
Marketing websites: ₹3-12 lakhs depending on scope and platform. E-commerce stores: ₹4-25 lakhs depending on catalogue size and customisation. Custom web applications: ₹8-50+ lakhs depending on functionality. Ongoing maintenance retainers: ₹15,000-1 lakh per month. We provide custom proposals after a discovery call and never quote without understanding actual scope.
