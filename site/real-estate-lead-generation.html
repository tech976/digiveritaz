Real estate buyers behave more like B2B enterprise purchasers than retail shoppers — they research for months, compare dozens of projects, and pull partners and parents into the final call. We design site-visit-first funnels for that reality.

Generalist agencies routinely fail in real estate because they apply consumer-funnel logic to a market where decisions are slow, ticket sizes are large, and sales teams reject low-quality leads on sight. We invest in qualification rigour at every stage — ad targeting, lead form, scoring, sales feedback loops — so your sales gallery isn't drowning in tyre-kickers.

Measurement

Spend is the input. Bookings are the output.

Six KPIs we report on every month so real estate marketing always ladders back to actual sales pipeline — not vanity lead counts.

01

Cost per lead (CPL)

By channel, by city and by configuration — so you can see exactly where the cheap leads come from and where they actually convert.

02

Walk-in CPA

Cost per confirmed site visit, not per form fill — the single most predictive metric of whether the campaign is buying real intent.

03

Walk-in to booking %

The sales-gallery conversion rate — measured by source so we know which channels send buyers who actually pay token money.

04

Cost per booking

All-in marketing cost divided by bookings — the only number the developer's finance team really wants on the monthly call.

05

Site visit ratio

Qualified site visits as a percentage of leads — the cleanest read on lead quality and the basis for every weekly optimisation.

06

Channel ROAS

Booking value divided by media spend, by channel — so portal, Google and Meta are always compared on actual revenue, not impressions.

FAQs

Questions developers ask before they sign.

Which real estate segments do you work with?
Residential developers, commercial real estate, luxury properties, affordable housing, plotted developments, and real estate brokerages. Our targeting and messaging strategies are adapted to each segment's specific buyer profile.
Do you run campaigns on Housing.com and MagicBricks as well?
We specialise in Google Ads, Meta Ads, and SEO for direct lead generation. We can advise on property portal strategies but focus our management on owned channel campaigns.
How do you ensure leads are serious buyers and not just curious browsers?
We layer qualification at multiple stages: ad-level targeting (income-correlated audiences, location and behaviour filters), lead-form qualification (budget range, timeline, configuration preference), automated lead scoring before sales handoff, and continuous feedback loops with your sales team. Cost per qualified site visit, not per lead, is the metric that matters.
What's a typical cost per site visit in Indian real estate?
It varies by city, segment, and project pricing. Luxury projects often see ₹3,000–₹8,000 per qualified site visit. Mid-segment ₹1,200–₹3,500. Affordable housing ₹400–₹1,500. We benchmark realistic targets after auditing your project, location, and competitive landscape.
Can you market both pre-launch and ready-to-move-in inventory?
Yes — these require fundamentally different funnels. Pre-launch projects need awareness, education, and early-bird booking incentives over a 6–12 month build cycle. Ready-to-move inventory needs immediate booking conversion campaigns with site visit emphasis. We design appropriate strategies for each stage.
