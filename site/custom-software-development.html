Off-the-shelf software solves the average business's problems. Custom software solves yours — the workflow quirks, the integrations, and the moat that no SaaS roadmap will ever ship for you.

We have rebuilt SaaS platforms that buckled at 1,000 paying customers, replaced thirteen-tool stacks with a single internal workspace, and shipped integration middleware that handles millions of events a day without losing one. The team is full-stack, in-house and stays with the product after launch — the part most agencies quietly outsource or walk away from.

For most businesses, SaaS is still the right answer. But when SaaS forces compromises that materially hurt the business, when the economics break above a certain user count, or when the capability simply does not exist on the market — custom is the right investment. We help you tell those cases apart before we write a line of code.

How we work

From discovery to scale — in five disciplined steps.

Five steps we run in lockstep so the software you launch is the software you can still trust at 10× the load.

01

Discovery & requirements

We sit with the people who will use the software, document the actual workflow, and write a requirements specification you can sign — including the non-functional ones (uptime, latency, audit, security).

02

Architecture & sprint plan

Stack selection, data model, API contracts, infrastructure diagram and a sprint plan with milestones, demos and a build budget — agreed before development starts.

03

MVP build

Two-week sprints, weekly demos, working software on staging from sprint one. CI, automated tests, code review and feature flags are part of the build — not an afterthought.

04

User testing & hardening

UAT with real users, load testing to expected and stretch volumes, security review and a closed bug list. Nothing ships to production until the punch list is empty.

05

Launch & scale

Staged rollout, on-call runbooks, monitoring and a maintenance retainer. We stay with the product through scale — bug fixes, feature additions, infrastructure operations.

FAQs

Questions teams ask before they sign.

How do you price custom software projects?
Custom software is scoped and priced based on a detailed requirements specification. We provide a fixed-price quote for well-defined projects and a time-and-materials arrangement for projects where requirements will evolve.
Do you provide ongoing maintenance after launch?
Yes. We provide ongoing support and development retainers covering bug fixing, performance monitoring, security updates, and new feature development.
Should we build custom or buy SaaS for our problem?
Almost always start with SaaS — off-the-shelf products are faster, cheaper, and better-supported than anything you build. Only consider custom when SaaS forces workflow compromises that materially hurt the business, when scale economics make per-user SaaS pricing prohibitive, or when functionality genuinely doesn't exist in any product on the market. Honest agencies steer you away from custom builds when SaaS would serve you.
What happens after the software is built?
Custom software needs ongoing operations — bug fixes, security patches, feature additions, infrastructure management, and adaptation to changing business needs. We offer maintenance retainers covering all of these, or we transition operations to your in-house team with documentation and training. Software without ongoing operations becomes liability software within 12-18 months.
